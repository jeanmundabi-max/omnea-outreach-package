The problem
Why procurement at growth-stage companies stays broken
Straight from the conversations CFOs and Procurement Heads are having right now.
01
"Coupa took 18 months and cost $2M in consulting fees. Half the company still doesn't use it."
Legacy procurement platforms promised transformation and delivered dependency. 18-month implementations. Armies of consultants. Configuration fees before a single purchase request is processed. And when it goes live, adoption sits at 30% because the interface requires training nobody has time for.
02
"80% of purchase requests still go through email and Slack because the approval system is too slow."
Maverick spend is not a discipline problem. It is a friction problem. When the approved process takes longer than asking a colleague on Slack, people route around it. Every purchase that bypasses the system is spend you cannot see until the invoice arrives.
03
"We have no visibility on what teams are actually spending until the invoice arrives."
The CFO wants spend visibility. The CPO wants compliance. The buyer just wants their laptop approved before the quarter ends. Without real-time orchestration, none of them get what they need. You are managing procurement with a rearview mirror.
04
"Another vendor told us they'd transform our procurement with AI. Show me a CFO who says their team actually uses it."
Every procurement vendor now claims AI. Coupa added AI. SAP added AI. The word means nothing without specifics. At most companies, AI in procurement means a chatbot that does not make decisions. Procurement teams have been burned enough times to require proof before they believe the pitch.
