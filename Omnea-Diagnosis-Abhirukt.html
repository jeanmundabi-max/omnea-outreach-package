The product wins every evaluation it enters. The gap is the system that determines how many evaluations Omnea enters in the first place.

The Core Leak

The product wins. Omnea achieved G2 Leader status in Procurement Orchestration, is ranked #1 for Results in Supplier Relationship Management, and has enterprise customers most procurement platforms spend years trying to land. The commercial motion that built this was sales-led and founder-network-led — which is exactly how you get to Series B.

It is not how you get to the next 10x.

The Founding Growth Marketing Lead job posting signals this directly. The word “Founding” means no growth marketing infrastructure exists. 161 employees, a US General Manager hired in January 2026, a new New York office — and no paid media programme, no comparison content, no Account Based Marketing motion, and no content engine capturing the enterprise procurement buyers who are right now evaluating Zip and Coupa as alternatives.

The gap is not product. The gap is the distribution layer that connects Omnea’s proof to the buyers who need it.
What This Seems to Indicate

The US expansion has no marketing infrastructure behind it

Arie Barendrecht joined as US General Manager in January 2026. The New York office is open. The US market requires a different go-to-market than the UK — content, paid channels, and SEO specific to US enterprise procurement buyers. Omnea is expanding into the US on relationships. The marketing layer that reaches buyers outside those relationships does not exist.

Zip and Coupa are capturing the comparison traffic Omnea should own

There is no “Omnea vs Zip” or “Omnea vs Coupa” comparison page. Buyers evaluating AI-native procurement alternatives search those terms. Omnea has a strong case — faster implementation, genuinely AI-native, better support ratings — and no content capturing that evaluation traffic.

The G2 proof is not in the demand gen motion

G2 Leader in Procurement Orchestration, #1 for Results in Supplier Relationship Management, Best Support, Best Relationship, Best Usability (Spring 2026) — category-defining proof points for enterprise procurement buyers. They live on a G2 badge. They are not in paid content, not in Account Based Marketing sequences, not in comparison pages.

The revenue proof is not in case study form

Spotify, MongoDB, Monzo, Wise, Albertsons — enterprise customers whose procurement teams chose Omnea over Coupa and SAP Ariba. None have a published case study with quantified outcomes. Enterprise buyers evaluating procurement platforms for the first time evaluate on peer proof. That proof is sitting in existing customer relationships, not in the marketing library.

Zip raised $43 million and has demand gen infrastructure that includes comparison content, LinkedIn advertising, events, and an SEO programme targeting every procurement keyword Omnea could own. Coupa has a marketing budget Omnea cannot match in brand spend — but Omnea can match in precision. Account Based Marketing targeting specific enterprise segments, comparison content capturing evaluation traffic, and a case study programme turning existing wins into pipeline fuel require precision, not budget scale.

Every month without a proof programme, Omnea generates customer wins that do not become pipeline assets. Every month without comparison content, enterprise evaluators searching for Zip and Coupa alternatives land on competitor pages. Every month without a US Account Based Marketing motion, the New York office grows relationships in a market where the marketing layer should already be generating inbound.

What MDB Runs First
1

Build the case study programme across the existing customer base

Spotify, MongoDB, Monzo, Wise, Albertsons — structured interviews extracting quantifiable outcomes: time from intake to approval, reduction in procurement overhead, implementation speed against Coupa benchmarks, spend under management growth. Turned into case studies, paid media assets, and Account Based Marketing content. MDB runs this as an ongoing programme — every new customer win becomes a marketing asset.

Produces: The peer proof library that enterprise procurement buyers need to shortlist Omnea over Coupa and SAP Ariba.
2

Build comparison content for US evaluation traffic

Dedicated comparison pages for Omnea versus Zip, Omnea versus Coupa, and an “AI-native procurement” alternatives page capturing buyers leaving legacy platforms. Built around the specific objections US enterprise finance and procurement leaders have — implementation complexity, AI depth, support quality, integration coverage. Optimised for the search terms buyers use when evaluating procurement orchestration for the first time.

Produces: Evergreen acquisition pages capturing high-intent evaluation traffic Omnea is currently losing to competitors.
3

Run a US enterprise Account Based Marketing programme

Paid LinkedIn campaigns targeting VP Procurement, CFO, and Head of Finance Operations at companies between 500 and 5,000 employees in technology, financial services, and retail — the segments Omnea’s existing customers confirm as ideal customer profile. Anchored on case study proof and G2 rankings. Not brand awareness. Evaluation-stage content that reaches buyers before they shortlist.

Produces: A running US demand gen motion that generates pipeline from the market Omnea’s Series B is designed to capture.
The Shift

Right now, Omnea grows through a sales motion and a founder network that got the company to Series B and the #1 UK Startup ranking. That is not the mechanism that gets to the next 10x in two markets. MDB is the engine that runs the growth marketing function — the case study programme, the comparison content, the US Account Based Marketing motion. The commercial team closes deals. MDB generates the pipeline they close.

Of the three gaps — the case study library that isn’t built, the comparison content that Zip is capturing, or the US Account Based Marketing motion that doesn’t exist — which one is costing Omnea the most pipeline right now?
